Commercial Window Cleaning Des Moines — A Property Manager's Guide

Why clean storefronts and office windows matter for your business — and how to choose the right cleaning partner.

Home/ Blog/ Commercial Window Cleaning Des Moines

Published: February 1, 2025 | By Window Worx

If you manage commercial property in Des Moines — whether it's a retail storefront, office building, restaurant, or medical office — your windows are making a statement every single day.

The question is: what are they saying about your business?

Dirty, streaky windows tell customers and tenants that you don't pay attention to details. Clean, sparkling windows say you care about your property and the people who use it. It's that simple.

Here's what Des Moines property managers and business owners need to know about commercial window cleaning — including how often to schedule, what it costs, and how to choose the right provider.

First Impressions Matter — Especially in Retail

Studies show that customers form an impression of a business within 7 seconds of seeing it. Dirty windows are one of the fastest ways to create a negative impression. Clean windows, on the other hand, signal professionalism, attention to detail, and pride in your business.

For retail and restaurant storefronts in Des Moines, clean windows directly impact foot traffic. A customer who can clearly see your products, your seating area, or your welcoming interior is far more likely to walk through the door.

Office Buildings and Tenant Satisfaction

For property managers of office buildings, window cleaning is about tenant retention. Natural light is one of the most requested amenities by office tenants. Dirty windows block natural light, making offices feel darker and less inviting — even on sunny days.

Regular commercial window cleaning keeps your building looking professional and helps tenants feel valued. It's a small investment that pays dividends in tenant satisfaction and lease renewals.

How Often Should Commercial Windows Be Cleaned?

The right frequency depends on your business type and location:

  • Retail storefronts and restaurants: Monthly or bi-monthly — high visibility and customer traffic demand clean windows
  • Office buildings: Quarterly is typical, with more frequent cleaning for ground-floor windows visible to the public
  • Medical and dental offices: Monthly — cleanliness and professionalism are critical in healthcare settings
  • Industrial and warehouse: Semi-annually, unless office areas face the public

What to Look for in a Commercial Window Cleaner

Commercial window cleaning is different from residential. Here's what to look for:

  • Commercial liability insurance — minimum $1M coverage
  • Workers' compensation — protects you from liability if a worker is injured on your property
  • Experience with commercial properties — storefronts, office buildings, and multi-story structures each have unique requirements
  • Flexible scheduling — after-hours or early morning cleaning to avoid disrupting your business
  • Consistent quality — you need the same crew or the same standards every time
  • Detailed proposals — clear scope of work, pricing, and frequency recommendations
