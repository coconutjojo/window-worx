Spring Window Cleaning Tips for Iowa Homeowners

How to shake off winter grime and get your windows sparkling for the warmer months ahead.

Home/ Blog/ Spring Window Cleaning Tips for Iowa Homeowners

Published: February 1, 2025 | By Window Worx

Iowa winters are brutal — on you, your home, and especially your windows. After months of snow, ice, road salt spray, and bitter cold, your windows have taken a beating. Spring is your chance to hit the reset button and get them looking their best again.

Here's your complete guide to spring window cleaning in Iowa — including when to schedule, what to expect from professional cleaning, and tips to keep your windows cleaner longer.

Why Spring is the Best Time for Window Cleaning

In Central Iowa, April through early June is the sweet spot for spring window cleaning. Here's why:

  • Winter grime removal: Road salt, sand, and road spray leave a gritty film on exterior windows that needs professional removal
  • Pollen season prep: Iowa's infamous pollen — that yellow-green dust that covers everything — bonds to dirty windows more easily than clean ones
  • Better light: Clean windows let in maximum spring sunshine, brightening your home after the dark winter months
  • Before summer heat: Getting windows cleaned before summer means you'll enjoy clear views during the months you actually want to look outside
  • Curb appeal: Spring is when neighbors are out, and first impressions matter — sparkling windows make your home stand out

What Iowa Winter Does to Your Windows

You might not realize how much damage winter causes because you're not looking at your windows up close in freezing temperatures. But trust us — it's significant:

  • Salt residue: Road salt spray dries on glass and leaves corrosive mineral deposits
  • Sand and grit: Plowed roads kick up fine sand that pits and scratches glass over time
  • Frozen condensation: Moisture freeze-thaw cycles can damage window seals and frames
  • Mold and mildew: Condensation trapped between storm windows and primary windows creates perfect breeding ground for mold

That's why a thorough spring cleaning isn't just cosmetic — it's protective maintenance for your windows.

DIY Spring Window Cleaning Tips

If you're tackling windows yourself this spring, follow these pro tips:

Use the Right Tools

Skip the paper towels and ammonia-based sprays. Invest in a good squeegee, microfiber cloths, and a mild detergent solution. For best results, use deionized or distilled water — tap water contains minerals that leave spots as it dries.

Clean in the Shade

Direct sunlight dries cleaning solution too quickly, leaving streaks. Clean windows on a cloudy day or on the shaded side of your house.

Don't Forget the Screens

Window screens trap incredible amounts of winter dust. Remove them, gently wash with mild soap and water, and let them air dry completely before reinstalling. Learn more about our screen cleaning service.

Clean Tracks and Sills

Vacuum out winter debris from window tracks and sills before washing the glass. Dirt in the tracks gets onto your cleaning tools and can scratch the glass.

When to Call the Pros

DIY cleaning works for some homes, but here's when it makes sense to hire a professional:

  • Two-story homes: Ladder work is dangerous and requires specialized equipment and training
  • Heavy buildup: Hard water stains, baked-on grime, and years of neglect require professional techniques
  • Large homes: Cleaning 25+ windows is an all-weekend project for DIYers — our crew finishes in a couple of hours
  • You want it done right: Professional results with streak-free, spotless glass every time
  • Window inspection: We spot issues you might miss — failed seals, rot, cracked glass — and notify you early
