Window Screen Repair vs. Replacement — Which Do You Need?

A homeowner's guide to dealing with damaged, torn, or worn-out window screens.

Home/ Blog/ Screen Repair vs. Replacement

Published: February 1, 2025 | By Window Worx

Window screens are easy to ignore — until there's a hole, a tear, or a sagging screen that lets bugs into your home. But when you finally inspect them, you might wonder: can this be repaired, or do I need a whole new screen?

At Window Worx, we handle screens as part of our regular window cleaning service, and we see every kind of screen situation imaginable. Here's our guide to help you decide between repair and replacement.

The Short Answer

  • Small tears and holes (under 2 inches): Can usually be patched or repaired
  • Large tears, multiple holes, or widespread damage: Better to replace the whole screen
  • Frayed or deteriorating screen mesh: Replace — the material is already failing
  • Screen popped out of the frame: Repair — just needs to be re-splined and re-seated
  • Bent or damaged frame: Check if the frame can be straightened; if not, full replacement needed
  • Screens that look dirty but are structurally fine: Professional screen cleaning is all you need

When to Repair Your Window Screen

Repair makes sense when the damage is minor and the screen material is still in good condition otherwise. Small holes from a stray baseball, a pet scratch, or an errant tree branch can usually be patched quickly.

DIY patch kits are available at hardware stores and work well for small repairs. They include a piece of screen mesh and adhesive or a small spline tool to weave the patch into place.

That said, patching is a temporary solution. If your screen has multiple patches or keeps developing new holes, it's time to replace the whole thing.

When to Replace Your Window Screen

Replacement is the better option when:

  • The mesh is old and brittle — sun exposure breaks down screen material over time, making it fragile
  • There are multiple large tears — patching a screen with extensive damage is like putting a Band-Aid on a broken arm
  • The frame is bent or warped — a damaged frame won't hold the screen tight, and bugs will find their way in
  • The spline (the rubber cord that holds the screen in the frame) is deteriorated — if the spline has cracked or shrunk, the screen will keep popping out
  • You want better visibility or airflow — newer screen materials offer improved clarity and better airflow than standard mesh

Don't Forget: Screens Need Regular Cleaning Too

Here's something most homeowners don't realize: dirty screens can look just as bad as torn screens.

Window screens trap dust, pollen, cobwebs, and even mold over time. This buildup blocks airflow, reduces natural light, and makes your windows look dirty even when the glass is spotless. Many homeowners think their screens are damaged when really they're just caked with years of grime.

We include professional screen cleaning and drying with every window cleaning service. Clean screens let more light in, improve air circulation when you open windows, and can extend the life of your screen mesh by removing corrosive debris. Learn more about our screen cleaning service.

What We See in Our Screen Inspections

Since we clean hundreds of homes every year, we've seen it all when it comes to screens:

  • Screens that are so dirty you can barely see through them (a thorough cleaning transforms them like magic)
  • Small tears that homeowners didn't know about (and were letting bugs inside)
  • Screen frames that are rusted or corroded (especially on older homes)
  • Pet damage — cats love scratching screens!
  • Screens installed incorrectly or in the wrong orientation

When we clean your windows, we inspect every screen. If we spot damage, we'll let you know and advise whether a simple cleaning, a repair, or a full replacement is the right call.

The Bottom Line

Window screens are an important part of your home's envelope — they keep bugs out while letting fresh air in. Keeping them clean and in good repair is a simple, affordable way to improve your home's comfort and energy efficiency.
