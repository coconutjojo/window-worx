DIY vs Professional Window Cleaning

Which approach is right for your home? We break down the costs, time, and results.

Home/ Blog/ DIY vs Professional Window Cleaning

Published: December 1, 2024 | By Window Worx

When your windows are looking dingy, you have two choices: grab a bucket and squeegee yourself, or call in the pros. But which is the better choice for your home in Waukee or the Des Moines metro?

We'll be honest — sometimes DIY makes sense. But there are clear advantages to professional cleaning that go beyond just convenience. Here's our honest breakdown.

The Case for DIY Window Cleaning

Cleaning your own windows can work in certain situations:

  • Lower cost per cleaning — You already own the supplies, so the marginal cost is just your time
  • Flexible schedule — Clean whenever it's convenient for you
  • Good for small jobs — A single window or two might not justify a service call
  • Sense of accomplishment — Some homeowners enjoy the hands-on work

But DIY has downsides: standard household cleaners often leave streaks, reaching second-story windows is dangerous, and most people simply don't have professional-grade tools or technique.

The Case for Professional Window Cleaning

Here's what you get when you hire Window Worx:

  • Streak-free results — Professional squeegee technique and purified water leave glass spotless
  • Safety — We have the equipment to safely reach high windows and tricky spots
  • Time savings — A professional two-person team can clean an entire home in 1-2 hours
  • Thoroughness — We clean tracks, sills, frames, and screens — not just the glass
  • Consistency — Every visit delivers the same high-quality results
  • Extended window life — Proper cleaning prevents damage and buildup that can degrade seals and frames

Cost Comparison

Let's talk numbers. A typical DIY setup (squeegee, scrubber, bucket, cleaning solution) costs around $30-50 upfront. Your time is worth something too — cleaning a typical 3-bedroom home takes 3-5 hours for an amateur.

A professional cleaning for that same home costs around $150-$400 depending on the number of windows, stories, and services included. When you factor in your time, the professional option is often more cost-effective — especially considering the superior results.

The Verdict

For most homeowners in the Des Moines metro, we recommend a hybrid approach: let the pros handle the deep cleaning 2-3 times per year, and do quick spot cleans between visits if needed. You get professional results for the big jobs without sacrificing the satisfaction of keeping things tidy in between.
