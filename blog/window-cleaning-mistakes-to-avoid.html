7 Window Cleaning Mistakes to Avoid

Common DIY window cleaning errors — and how to fix them, according to the pros.

Home/ Blog/ 7 Window Cleaning Mistakes to Avoid

Published: February 1, 2025 | By Window Worx

We've been cleaning windows professionally since 2009, and we've seen just about every DIY mistake you can imagine. Some are harmless (but frustrating). Others can actually damage your windows permanently.

Here are the 7 most common window cleaning mistakes we see — and how to avoid them.

Mistake #1: Cleaning Windows in Direct Sunlight

This is the #1 cause of streaks. When the sun is beating down on your windows, cleaning solution evaporates almost instantly — leaving soap residue and mineral deposits behind before you can squeegee it off.

The fix: Clean windows on a cloudy day, early in the morning, or in the evening. Work on the shaded side of your house first.

Mistake #2: Using Paper Towels or Ragged Cloths

Paper towels leave lint. Old t-shirts and towels can be dirty or have fabric softener residue that smears. Newspaper (an old-school trick) works better than both, but it's messy and the ink can transfer to your window frames.

The fix: Use a professional-grade squeegee with a clean, lint-free microfiber cloth for edging. It's the only way to guarantee streak-free results.

Mistake #3: Using the Wrong Cleaning Solution

Ammonia-based cleaners (like Windex) can damage window tints, certain window films, and some modern glass coatings. Dish soap leaves a film that attracts more dirt over time. And vinegar — while popular among DIYers — doesn't cut through hard water stains or heavy grime effectively.

The fix: Use a professional-grade window cleaning solution or a mild detergent specifically designed for glass. And always use deionized or distilled water — tap water contains minerals that leave spots as they dry.

Mistake #4: Scrubbing Dirt Into the Glass

When windows are really dirty, it's tempting to scrub hard. But that just grinds abrasive grit into the glass, causing micro-scratches that accumulate over time and make windows look hazy.

The fix: Use a soft microfiber cloth or professional scrubber, and rinse frequently. For heavy buildup, let the cleaning solution sit for a minute to loosen dirt before wiping. Better yet, let a professional handle windows that need serious cleaning.

Mistake #5: Forgetting the Tracks and Sills

You can have spotless glass, but if the window tracks are caked with dirt, debris, and dead bugs, your windows still look dirty. Plus, that debris holds moisture against the frame, promoting rot and mold.

The fix: Vacuum tracks and sills before washing the glass. Use a small brush or attachment to get into corners. Wipe down with a damp cloth.

Mistake #6: Not Cleaning Screens (Or Cleaning Them Wrong)

Dirty screens block airflow and light — and when you reinstall them on clean windows, they make the windows look dirty again. But many homeowners either skip screen cleaning entirely or scrub them too aggressively, damaging the mesh.

The fix: Remove screens, gently wash with mild soap and water using a soft brush, rinse thoroughly, and let them dry completely before reinstalling. We include professional screen cleaning with every window cleaning service — learn more here.

Mistake #7: Using the Same Dirty Tools on Multiple Windows

Your squeegee and cloth pick up dirt from the first window. If you use them on the second window without rinsing, you're just spreading that dirt around.

The fix: Rinse your squeegee blade and change your cloths frequently. Professionals carry multiple clean cloths and change them often throughout the job.
